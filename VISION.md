# Vision

## Origin

This site started as a digital CV — a place to present professional experience,
education, and volunteering in a clean, interactive format. It served that
purpose well, but it no longer reflects the full scope of what the site should
communicate.

## Purpose

This website is Simon Amtoft Pedersen's personal professional hub. Its purpose
is to establish a strong, coherent professional presence that positions Simon as
a trusted expert in data science and AI — not just someone with a solid track
record, but someone worth reaching out to.

This is a personal site. Employers and roles are part of the story, but the site
represents Simon as an individual — independent of any single organisation.

The site should convey:

- **Hands-on technical depth** in ML Engineering, Data Science, and MLOps —
  grounded in real-world experience and engineering rigour. This is the core.
- **Generative AI and agents** as a secondary but real capability area — applied
  in production, not just as a talking point.
- **A consulting and advisory mindset** — the ability to bridge complex business
  problems and AI solutions that actually get adopted, acting as a trusted
  technical advisor who also does the hands-on work.
- **Active community involvement** — publishing, speaking, organising, and
  contributing to the broader data and AI community.

## Audience

The site is written in English and positioned for an international audience.
It serves several groups, roughly in order of priority:

1. **Potential clients and decision-makers** — people evaluating whether to
   engage Simon for data and AI work.
2. **Industry peers and collaborators** — fellow practitioners who may want to
   collaborate, exchange ideas, or invite Simon to speak or contribute.
3. **The broader professional community** — conference attendees, readers of
   published articles, and others exploring the data and AI space.
4. **Recruiters and employers** — people assessing Simon's background and fit
   for opportunities.

## What Success Looks Like

The vision is fulfilled when:

- **Professional credibility is immediate.** When someone searches for Simon
  online, they find a clear, professional presence that accurately represents
  his expertise and involvement in the field.
- **Opportunities come inbound.** The site generates inquiries — potential
  clients, collaboration proposals, speaking invitations — without Simon having
  to pitch himself.

## Guiding Principles

These principles should inform decisions about what to build, how to present
content, and what to prioritise:

1. **Substance over style.** Every section should communicate real competence.
   Avoid generic filler and buzzwords.
2. **Show, don't just list.** Where possible, demonstrate expertise through
   context — publications, talks, community roles — rather than listing skills
   and job titles.
3. **Professional but approachable.** The tone should be confident and direct,
   without being stiff or self-promotional.
4. **Confidentiality-aware.** Most project work is confidential. The site should
   convey depth of experience without exposing client-specific details.
5. **Evolve incrementally.** The site will grow over time. New content types may
   be added as they become relevant. The architecture should support this
   without requiring rewrites.
